# Design System: SCSDA

## Overview

The site should feel like a careful research-institution website: formal,
legible, calm, and grounded in real SCSDA public material. It is not a planning
memo, personal portfolio, marketing landing page, or explanation of why the
site exists.

## Visual Direction

- Use real SCSDA images, event records, research-space photos, and engineering
  project visuals.
- Keep pages light and readable with restrained teal and gold accents.
- Use serif display headings for academic gravity and a modern sans stack for
  long-form readability.
- Keep cards flat, with 8px radius and one-pixel borders.
- Reserve shadows for image panels only.

## Content Rules

- Write as a formal academy website.
- Keep SCSDA as the first subject.
- Present Dr Han through leadership and platform-development responsibilities.
- Mark 2019-2023 figures as construction-period records.
- Exclude confidential, military, surveillance, public-security, and
  government-decision wording.
- Avoid meta-copy that says what a reader should infer.

## Components

- **Hero:** full-width real image background, direct institutional headline.
- **Navigation:** compact text navigation, no decorative controls.
- **Metrics:** period-labelled and source-bounded.
- **Research cards:** theme, summary, scope.
- **Project cards:** real image, category, public summary, short bullet list.
- **Callouts:** only for risk boundaries or important context, not for
  meta-explanations about why the site should persuade a reader.
