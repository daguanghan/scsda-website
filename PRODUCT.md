# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary users are UK and European academic reviewers, search committees,
research collaborators and professional contacts who need a concise,
credible English account of Smart Cities and Sustainable Development Academy.
They are scanning for evidence of Dr Daguang Han's research-platform
leadership, applied research translation and academic relevance in digital
built environments, smart infrastructure, BIM, digital twins, sensing and
sustainable urban systems.

## Product Purpose

This website presents SCSDA as an institutional research-platform website, not
as a personal-only profile. Its success criterion is that a cautious academic
reviewer can understand the Academy's nonprofit applied-research positioning,
then infer Dr Han's founding and executive leadership through source-bounded,
non-confidential institutional evidence.

## Brand Personality

Careful, institutional, evidence-led. The site should feel like a serious
research platform with enough visual quality to support international academic
review, while avoiding the tone of marketing exaggeration or personal
promotion.

## Anti-references

Do not make the site look like a generic AI-generated SaaS landing page, a
personal vanity portfolio, a government authority site, a public-security
platform, or a decorative dark-tech brochure. Avoid purple-blue gradients,
glassmorphism, nested card-heavy layouts, fake dashboards, over-rounded cards,
hero statistic templates, vague innovation claims and repeated small uppercase
section labels.

## Design Principles

1. Institution first, person evidenced inside the institution.
2. Every claim should have a boundary: public-facing, non-confidential and
   source-bounded.
3. Academic credibility beats visual excitement.
4. Research themes should map to UK and European faculty-review priorities.
5. Historical Chinese-site material can be reused, but only after filtering for
   international readability and risk.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and readable mobile layouts. Motion should be
restrained and never required for comprehension. Navigation should remain
plain, keyboard-accessible and understandable to reviewers who are scanning
quickly rather than exploring like consumers.
