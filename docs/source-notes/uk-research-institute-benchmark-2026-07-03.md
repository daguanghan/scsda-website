# UK Research Institute Benchmarks for SCSDA

This note compares UK-facing research institutes and applied innovation bodies
that are relevant to SCSDA's public positioning: smart cities, digital built
environment, infrastructure sensing, urban data, sustainability and applied
research translation.

## Summary

The strongest UK examples do not rely on exaggerated language. They make the
institution credible through structure: research themes, people, facilities,
projects, outputs, news, funders or partners, and clear collaboration routes.
For SCSDA, the priority is to present a complete institutional website while
keeping claims measured and source-bounded.

## Comparable UK Examples

| Institution | Relevant Pattern | What SCSDA Should Learn |
|---|---|---|
| UCL Bartlett Centre for Advanced Spatial Analysis | Interdisciplinary institute focused on the science of cities; site sections include Study, Research, People, About, Publications, News and Events. | SCSDA should group urban analytics, smart cities, sensing and digital built environment work into clear research themes, then support them with people, projects and outputs. |
| Cambridge Centre for Smart Infrastructure and Construction | Civil-engineering research group specialising in smart infrastructure and construction with data-centric, whole-life systems framing, people pages, funder pages and partner/membership material. | SCSDA should foreground smart infrastructure, sensing, BIM/digital twins and lifecycle monitoring as a coherent civil-engineering platform, not as disconnected product pages. |
| Connected Places Catapult | UK innovation accelerator for transport, built environment, cities and local growth; has clear "what we do", projects, work-with-us, careers and resources sections. | SCSDA can adopt the applied-innovation structure: explain research-to-practice pathways, show demonstrators, and provide collaboration/join/message entry points. |
| Newcastle Urban Observatory | Urban data and sensing network with real-time sensor measurements, images, remote-sensing data and smart-building data; public records emphasise scale and open data. | SCSDA should present sensing, AIoT, monitoring and dashboards as a research-data capability, with technical examples and careful descriptions of public-facing workflows. |
| University of Bristol Cabot Institute for the Environment | Interdisciplinary institute organised around major environmental themes such as water, low carbon energy, environmental change, city futures and natural hazards. | SCSDA should keep sustainability as a cross-cutting research theme, not only a slogan, and connect it to construction, infrastructure and urban-system questions. |

## Website Upgrade Implications

SCSDA should evolve toward a UK-readable research-institute structure:

- Home: clear institutional identity, research areas, platform indicators, and selected public evidence.
- About: origin, mission, platform-construction story, institutional principles and media record.
- Leadership: founder and founding executive lead, expert committee, centre leadership and collaboration network.
- Research: 4-6 research themes with method, scope and technical relevance.
- Projects: selected case studies and demonstrators, written as public applied-research summaries.
- Outputs: period-labelled indicators, papers, IP, R&D projects, training and incubation activity.
- People or Network: founder, expert advisers, centre leads and collaborators, without inflating current staffing.
- Join / Message: professional opportunities and collaboration enquiries, phrased like a research institute rather than a consumer website.
- News: later add updates, talks, reports and public project notes.

## Design Implications

- Use technical, platform, equipment, dashboard and project images.
- Avoid reception, signing-event and government-visit images as primary evidence.
- Keep page widths, heading sizes, card heights and image ratios consistent.
- Avoid visible planning language such as "for UK applications", "evidence layer", or "why this proves".
- Use measured institutional language: "applied research platform", "public-facing research summaries", "research-centre development", "engineering demonstrators", "education-oriented knowledge exchange".
